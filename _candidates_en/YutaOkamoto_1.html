"Reactive" is now attracting the attention in various fields from the programming model to the architecture of distributed systems. ReactiveX, the asynchronous streaming API, and Reactive Manifesto, the design principles of large-scale systems promoted by Typesafe, are both famous examples of the reactive technologies.

And the language standard is now starting to accept the reactiveness. Java 9 plans to add the specification of asynchronous back-pressure, Reactive Streams, to its standard library.

However, why does every technology coming from the completely different background need similar concept? "Why Functional Programming Matters" written by John Hughes in the 1980s will give you a hint.

In this session, I would like to look at "why reactive matters" as a means of promoting modularity of our code, system and organization.
