Since original iPhone launched in 2007, Apple was asking developers to use their Objective-C language to make iPhone applications.

In June 2014, that situation was drastically changed in good way for us -- Swift language. A new shiny functional programing language, Swift has many similarities to Scala like mixture of functional and objective oriented style etc.

As experiences of both working on backend services in Scala and iOS applications for many years at Twitter who loves Scala a lot, I'll give a presentation that invites you all to iOS world with Scala.
